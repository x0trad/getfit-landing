## Legal & compliance — read before publishing

These are flagged because a GLP-1/telehealth page carries real regulatory risk. Have your own counsel and medical/compliance reviewers approve the final copy.

1. **Write your own everything.** Don't copy MEDVi's (or any competitor's) testimonials, photos, claims, addresses, or disclaimer text. The copy above is original and generic; the placeholders are where your real, verified details go.
2. **Substantiate every claim.** Stats like "X% average weight loss" and "kept the weight off" need to come from your own data, with a citation and an "individual results vary" disclaimer.
3. **Trademarks.** Wegovy®, Ozempic®, Zepbound® etc. belong to their manufacturers. Only mention them if you actually offer them, and include a trademark-ownership disclaimer.
4. **Compounded medication disclosures.** If you offer compounded GLP-1s, you typically must disclose that compounded drugs aren't FDA-approved, and explain the provider/telehealth relationship. Use your own legally reviewed language.
5. **No doctor-patient relationship by quiz.** Standard disclaimer that completing the assessment doesn't create a doctor-patient relationship.
6. **Provider/pharmacy partners.** List only real, licensed partners with their correct details and required disclosures.
7. **Guarantees & refunds.** Only state a guarantee you actually honor, and link a real refund policy.
8. **HSA/FSA, "free" claims, insurance language.** Only claim what's true; add the eligibility footnotes.
